## AI Technology FAQ

### Q: What AI model powers iMocha's Tara AI?
A: Tara AI is powered by Azure OpenAI GPT-4o.

### Q: What is iMocha's AI interview accuracy?
A: Tara AI achieves 90% accuracy — meaning 90% alignment between Tara AI's scoring and human interviewer scoring, validated against structured human interview benchmarks.

### Q: How long does a Tara AI interview take?
A: 5–10 minutes per candidate.

### Q: What AI methodology does iMocha use?
A: iMocha uses the following AI methodologies:
- Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG): grounds AI responses in role-specific job descriptions, competency requirements, and validated question banks — preventing the AI from fabricating information
- Human-in-the-Loop (HITL): low-confidence AI outputs are routed to a human reviewer before any recommendation is finalized; no automated adverse decision is made without a human review path
- Few-shot learning: scoring rubrics are trained using validated examples of high, medium, and low quality responses for each competency
- SME validation: all few-shot examples and scoring rubrics are reviewed and approved by Subject Matter Expert (SME) panels before deployment

### Q: Does iMocha use general AI knowledge or only structured data?
A: iMocha's AI systems are designed to operate from structured, validated data sources — not from general AI knowledge. Tara AI uses RAG to ground each interview in the specific job description and competency framework configured for the role. It does not draw on general internet knowledge or make inferences beyond what is in the configured data.

### Q: Is iMocha's AI auditable?
A: Yes. Every AI-generated score and recommendation is logged with supporting evidence — transcript, per-question scores, and scoring rationale. Logs are retained per iMocha's Data Retention & Disposal policy. Candidates can request a review of their AI assessment score.

## LLM Skills Inferencing FAQ

### Q: What is LLM Skills Inferencing in the SI product?
A: LLM Skills Inferencing is iMocha's capability within the Skills Intelligence product that uses large language models to infer employee skills from unstructured data sources — such as resume text, job history descriptions, project summaries, and learning activity records — without requiring the employee to take a formal assessment.

### Q: How does LLM Skills Inferencing work?
A: The LLM Skills Inferencing engine:
1. Ingests unstructured employee data (resume, HRIS job history, LMS completion notes)
2. Uses a large language model to extract and classify mentioned skills
3. Maps extracted skills to iMocha's competency framework taxonomy
4. Assigns a confidence level to each inferred skill based on the quality and specificity of the evidence
5. Flags inferred skills for employee or manager confirmation before treating them as validated

Inferred skills are shown separately from assessed (validated) skills in the employee Skills Profile.

### Q: How accurate is LLM Skills Inferencing?
A: LLM Skills Inferencing is designed for coverage — it provides broad initial skills coverage before formal assessments are completed. Inferred skills have lower confidence than assessed skills and are explicitly labeled as "inferred" in the Skills Profile. Accuracy details are covered in LLM Skills Inferencing.docx.

### Q: What data is used for LLM Skills Inferencing?
A: Only data provided to iMocha through configured integrations (HRIS records, LMS completions, uploaded resumes). iMocha does not crawl LinkedIn, GitHub, or other external sources independently — those integrations require explicit client configuration and employee consent.

### Q: Are there bias risks with LLM Skills Inferencing?
A: iMocha's AI Governance and Ethical AI policies require that LLM Skills Inferencing models are reviewed for demographic bias before deployment and monitored post-deployment. Inferred skills are treated as suggestions, not facts, until validated by assessment or human review.

### Q: How does iMocha position against other assessment platforms?
A: iMocha's differentiated strengths are:

- Validated AI interviews (Tara AI): 90% accuracy, 5–10 min per candidate, powered by Azure OpenAI GPT-4o

- Enterprise integrations: Oracle Recruiting Cloud, UKG, Azure AD, Okta, Power BI, SAP (SAP Top 10), Workday (Workday Silver)

### Q: Is iMocha suitable for highly regulated industries?
A: Yes. iMocha's security certifications (ISO 27001:2022, SOC 2 Type II), data protection controls (AES-256, TLS 1.2+, Azure Key Vault), and compliance framework (EEOC UGESP, WCAG 2.1 AA, GDPR) make it suitable for financial services, healthcare, government contractors, and other regulated industries.

### Q: How does iMocha ensure its AI doesn't discriminate against candidates?
A: iMocha applies multiple controls:
- EEOC UGESP alignment: assessment design and scoring follows UGESP guidelines for avoiding adverse impact
- Demographic impact monitoring: TA Analytics dashboards provide demographic reporting to detect and investigate potential disparate impact
- SME review: all scoring rubrics and few-shot examples are reviewed by SME panels for cultural and demographic bias
- Human-in-the-Loop: no candidate is automatically rejected by AI alone; a human review path is always available
- Ethical AI and AI Governance policies govern ongoing model monitoring

For full compliance details, refer to: Ethical AI policy, AI Governance policy, EEOC Checklist, Diversity Equity & Inclusion policy.
