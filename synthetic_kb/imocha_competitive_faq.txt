## How iMocha Compares to Key Competitors

### iMocha vs. HackerRank

**Summary**: iMocha is the broader enterprise platform; HackerRank is the developer-focused coding assessment tool.

| Dimension | iMocha | HackerRank |
|-----------|--------|------------|
| Assessment breadth | 3,000+ tests (coding, cognitive, behavioral, ERP, language) | Primarily coding/technical |
| Skills Intelligence | Full Skills Intelligence Cloud with workforce planning | Limited to developer skills |
| Proctoring | AI proctoring with video, audio, behavior analytics | Basic proctoring |
| Live interviews | CodeSpace (collaborative IDE + whiteboard) | CodePair |
| HRIS/ATS integrations | 20+ native integrations | 10–15 integrations |
| On-premises deployment | Yes (Enterprise) | No |
| Language support | 40+ coding languages + 5 spoken languages | 30+ coding languages |
| Behavioral assessments | Yes (DiSC, SJT, EQ, Leadership) | No |
| Enterprise pricing | $80K–$500K+ | $40K–$250K+ |

When to choose iMocha over HackerRank:
- Client needs beyond developer assessment (e.g., non-tech roles, behavioral, language)
- Client wants workforce intelligence + skills gap analysis, not just hiring assessments
- Client needs SAP/Oracle/Salesforce ERP assessments
- Client requires on-premises or dedicated tenant deployment

When HackerRank might win:
- Pure developer hiring with smaller scale (engineering-only use case)
- Client already using GitHub Classroom or GitHub Copilot ecosystem
- Lower budget, simpler needs

### iMocha vs. Codility

**Summary**: Codility focuses narrowly on algorithmic coding challenges for software engineers; iMocha is a full-spectrum talent assessment platform.

Key differences:
- Codility's question library is almost exclusively algorithmic problems (competitive programming style)
- iMocha's coding assessments include real-world scenarios: build a REST API, debug a Docker config, write a Terraform module
- Codility has no behavioral or cognitive assessment capability
- iMocha's proctoring is significantly more advanced than Codility's
- Codility has strong brand recognition among engineering managers who went through it as candidates; iMocha is stronger at HR/TA buyer level

Talk track when Mettl is the incumbent:

### iMocha vs. Vervoe

**Summary**: Vervoe focuses on work-sample assessments for SMB/mid-market; iMocha is enterprise-focused with deeper skills intelligence.

- Vervoe's differentiator: AI-graded open-ended responses and short video answers; strong for customer service, sales, and operations roles
- iMocha advantage: deeper technical depth, HRIS integration, skills ontology, on-premises
- Vervoe advantage: faster setup (hours vs days), lower price point, better candidate experience design

---

### iMocha vs. Pymetrics (Harver)

**Summary**: Pymetrics/Harver focuses on neuroscience-based game assessments for behavioral fit and bias reduction; different from iMocha's skills-based approach.

- Pymetrics strengths: high-quality behavioral/cognitive games, strong DE&I story, good for volume hiring (retail, banking frontline)
- iMocha strengths: technical skills validation, coding assessments, skills intelligence
- These tools are often complementary (some clients use Pymetrics for behavioral top-of-funnel + iMocha for technical validation)

## Frequently Asked Sales Questions

### Pricing & Commercial

**Q: Why is iMocha more expensive than some competitors?**
A: iMocha's price reflects the depth of the platform — not just assessment delivery, but skills intelligence, workforce planning, and validated psychometric quality. When you factor in the total cost of hiring mistakes, skills mismatches, and unoptimized L&D spend, customers consistently achieve positive ROI within 12–18 months. Our average contract expansion rate is 127% (customers expand usage after seeing initial value).

**Q: Can we do a pilot or proof of concept before signing?**
A: Yes. iMocha offers a structured 30-day PoC with:
- Up to 5 assessments deployed
- Up to 200 candidate invitations
- ATS integration with one system
- Full reporting access
- Success criteria defined upfront (e.g., reduce screening time by 40%, achieve 80% candidate satisfaction)
PoC pricing: $3,000–$5,000 (credited toward annual contract if client signs).

**Q: Do you offer month-to-month pricing?**
A: No, iMocha's standard minimum commitment is 12 months. Month-to-month is not available for SaaS. On-premises contracts require a minimum 2-year term.

**Q: What happens if we exceed our assessment volume?**
A: Overage pricing applies. Standard overage rate: 20–30% above per-assessment rate in your contract. Enterprise contracts typically include 10% buffer with no overage charge.

### Technical Questions

**Q: Can iMocha integrate with our custom in-house ATS?**
A: Yes, via the iMocha REST API. We provide a full OpenAPI 3.0 specification, SDK libraries (Python, JS, Java, .NET), and implementation support. Timeline for custom integration: 2–6 weeks depending on complexity.

**Q: Does iMocha support SSO with Azure AD?**
A: Yes. iMocha supports SAML 2.0 and OIDC. Azure AD is fully supported with step-by-step configuration guides. Setup typically takes 30–60 minutes with your IT team.

**Q: Can we white-label the candidate assessment experience?**
A: Yes, all client plans include customizable branding on the candidate-facing portal: logo, colors, custom subdomain (e.g., assessments.yourcorp.com), welcome message, and completion redirect URL. Full white-labeling (removing iMocha branding entirely) is available for Enterprise+ plans.

**Q: How does the AI proctoring handle candidates with disabilities?**
A: iMocha provides accommodation modes:
- Extended time (configurable per candidate)
- Screen reader mode (WCAG 2.1 AA compliant)
- Disable gaze tracking (for candidates with visual impairments)
- Human review instead of AI for all flagged sessions (when client enables accommodation mode)
Disability accommodations do not affect the scoring algorithm.

**Q: What coding languages are supported for live coding interviews?**
A: CodeSpace supports 42 languages including: Python 3.11, Java 21, JavaScript (Node 20), TypeScript, C++17, C#, Go 1.22, Rust, Kotlin, Swift, Ruby, PHP, Scala, R, MATLAB, and all major SQL dialects. New language versions are added within 30 days of LTS release.

**Q: Can candidates take assessments on mobile devices?**
A: Yes, all non-coding assessments are fully mobile-responsive (iOS Safari 16+, Chrome Android 110+). Coding assessments are not recommended on mobile (code editor UX degrades on small screens). Clients can restrict assessments to desktop-only via configuration.

**Q: How accurate is iMocha's AI proctoring?**
A: Based on internal validation studies:
- True positive rate (correctly flagging actual cheating): 84%
- False positive rate (flagging legitimate behavior as suspicious): 7%
- All flags are soft flags — no candidate is automatically disqualified without human review
- Clients can configure flag sensitivity (default: medium; options: low, medium, high, strict)

### Assessment Quality

**Q: How do we know iMocha's questions are current and not outdated?**
A: iMocha's content governance process:
1. All questions are timestamped with a "review by" date (typically 18 months from creation)
2. Domain-specific review cycles: cloud questions reviewed every 6 months (fast-moving); behavioral questions reviewed annually
3. Item analytics: questions with low discrimination index (<0.2) or extreme difficulty (<20% or >85% pass rate) are flagged for replacement
4. Client feedback loop: recruiters and hiring managers can flag specific questions; flagged questions reviewed within 5 business days

**Q: Can we use our own questions / IP in iMocha?**
A: Yes. The custom question editor supports:
- Uploading questions via CSV bulk import
- Authoring directly in the platform
- Mixing client questions with iMocha library questions in the same assessment
- Marking client questions as proprietary (not accessible by iMocha for QA or benchmarking)

**Q: Do candidates get feedback after the assessment?**
A: Configurable per assessment and per plan:
- Default: candidates see "submitted" confirmation only (no score, no answers)
- Optional: show overall score and section scores after submission
- Optional: show correct answers and explanations (recommended for internal development, not for competitive hiring)
- Full report: available to candidates via share link if client enables this

**Q: How long does it take to get assessment results?**
A:
- Objective questions (MCQ, coding auto-graded): instant results (within 60 seconds of submission)
- Video responses (AI-graded): 5–15 minutes
- Video responses (human review queue): 24–48 hours (business hours)
- Full proctoring review (flagged sessions): 24–48 hours for human review completion

### Implementation & Support

**Q: We have a strict deadline — can we go live in 2 weeks?**
A: For SaaS with no custom integrations: yes. Standard onboarding checklist:
- Day 1: Account provisioned, SSO configured, first admin trained
- Day 3–5: ATS integration via standard connector (Greenhouse/Lever) or webhook
- Day 7: First assessments imported/built and tested
- Day 10: Pilot with 10 internal candidates

For Workday or SAP SuccessFactors integration: minimum 3–4 weeks due to client IT dependencies.

**Q: What languages is the admin portal available in?**
A: English, French, Spanish, German, Portuguese (Brazil), Hindi, and Simplified Chinese. The candidate portal supports the same languages plus Dutch, Italian, Polish, Turkish, and Japanese.

**Q: Do you have a mobile app for recruiters?**
A: Not currently. The admin/recruiter portal is web-only (responsive for tablet use). A native iOS/Android app is on the H2 2025 roadmap. Candidates do not need an app — assessments run in mobile browser.

**Q: What is iMocha's NPS?**
A: Last measured NPS (Q4 2024): +48 (HR/TA buyers) and +31 (candidates). Industry benchmark for HR Tech SaaS: +32. iMocha consistently outperforms benchmark on the buyer side.
